# 📊 ITC Financial Model & Valuation

This project presents a complete financial model and valuation analysis of **ITC Limited**, one of India's largest diversified FMCG companies. The model forecasts ITC’s financial performance and estimates the intrinsic value of the company using **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)** and **Comparable Company Analysis**.

The objective of this project is to build a fully integrated financial model and analyze whether ITC’s stock is overvalued or undervalued based on its future cash flows.

------------------------------------------------------------

🏢 COMPANY OVERVIEW

ITC Limited is a diversified Indian conglomerate operating in multiple business segments including:

• 🚬 Cigarettes  
• 🛍️ FMCG (Foods and Personal Care)  
• 🌾 Agri Business  
• 📦 Paperboards and Packaging  
• 🏨 Hotels  

The company generates strong cash flows from its cigarette business while expanding rapidly in the FMCG segment.

------------------------------------------------------------

🎯 PROJECT OBJECTIVE

The objective of this project is to:

• Build a fully integrated **three-statement financial model**  
• Forecast ITC’s financial performance  
• Calculate **Free Cash Flow**  
• Estimate intrinsic value using **Discounted Cash Flow**  
• Compare valuation using **peer companies**

------------------------------------------------------------

🧠 MODEL STRUCTURE

The financial model includes the following components:

📄 Financial Statements

• Income Statement  
• Balance Sheet  
• Cash Flow Statement  

⚙️ Forecast Drivers

• Revenue growth assumptions  
• Cost assumptions  
• Working capital assumptions  

📑 Supporting Schedules

• Revenue Schedule  
• Cost Schedule  
• Working Capital Schedule  
• Capex Schedule  
• Debt Schedule  
• Tax Schedule  
• Equity Schedule  

📈 Valuation Analysis

• Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)  
• Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)  
• Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

------------------------------------------------------------

💰 VALUATION METHODS USED

📌 1. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

Two approaches were used:

• Perpetuity Growth Method  
• Exit Multiple Method  

DCF estimates the intrinsic value of the company based on projected future free cash flows.

📊 2. Comparable Company Analysis

Peer companies used for comparison include:

• Hindustan Unilever  
• Nestle India  
• Britannia Industries  
• Marico  
• Dabur India  
• Godrej Consumer  

Valuation multiples used:

• EV / Revenue  
• EV / EBITDA  
• Price / Earnings (P/E)

------------------------------------------------------------

🔑 KEY ASSUMPTIONS

• Forecast Period: **2026 – 2030**  
• Terminal Growth Rate: **4%**  
• WACC: **12.6%**  
• Corporate Tax Rate: **25%**

Revenue growth is forecast separately for:

• Cigarettes  
• FMCG  
• Agriculture  
• Paperboards

📊 MODEL OUTPUTS

The model generates:

• Enterprise Value  
• Equity Value  
• Intrinsic Value per Share  
• Sensitivity Analysis  
• Football Field Valuation Chart  

These outputs help evaluate whether the stock is **overvalued or undervalued** relative to its current market price.
